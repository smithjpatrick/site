Toast

My team and I designed a self-ordering kiosk interface to help Toast generate revenue through increased adoption of their kiosk product. From research with stakeholders and usability testing, we designed a solution that helps patrons save time and delivers key features requested by restaurants.

Client Challenge

Toast, Inc. is a producer of point-of-sale hardware and software systems for restaurants. They wanted to increase the adoption of their self-order kiosks to capitalize on the growing demand for kiosk technology in the food service industry. In order to increase restaurant adoption and generate revenue, Toast enlisted the help of my team and me to understand the needs of both restaurants and patrons with respect to kiosk usage and deliver a solution to address them.

Our Solution

After an initial client meeting and interviews with a restaurant manager and patrons, my team learned that a successful design needed to support the goals of not just the end user, but also the actual customer of the kiosk itself - the restaurant owner or manager. To that end, our design focused on delivering improvements in the following areas:

  • Time-savings. One of the primary reasons one may use a kiosk to order is to skip a long line and save themselves time, so our kiosk helps patrons get in and out more quickly.
  • Personalization. A preferred method of ordering is using one’s own mobile device. It’s more personal and allows users to act upon set preferences. We wanted to take advantage of purchasing history from repeat customers to provide a level of personalization on a public device.
  • Item modification. One of the top requests from restaurants is improved menu modifications, to cater to patron needs and also help generate revenue for restaurants.
  • Upselling. Kiosks have a higher potential to increase average check sizes, so we wanted to implement upselling opportunities that were appropriate and did not feel overly obtrusive.

My Role

Throughout the project, I was actively involved with each stage of our research and design efforts:

  • Conducting field interviews with restaurant manager and patrons
  • SWOT analysis
  • Sketching and creating low-fidelity wireframes
  • Usability testing our paper prototype
  • Creating high-fidelity interactive prototype
  • Presenting our design solution to the client group

During the project, I took the lead developing the interview script and conducting the interview with the restaurant manager, developing the item customization prototype, and delivering the final presentation and design walkthrough to the clients.
