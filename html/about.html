About me.

I love finding creative solutions to diverse problems. Whether I was designing pipelines under major highways in my past life as an engineer or designing a great ordering experience at restaurants as a UX designer, I get really excited tackling tough problems to help others.

Starting out

I actually began designing as a civil engineer, devising system improvements for a natural gas utility. I gained a lot of experience and skills from this role that have helped me as a UX designer, like:

  • breaking down a problem.
  • gathering data and using it to inform decision-making.
  • iterating through ideas based on stakeholder feedback.
  • communicating the value of my work effectively to multi-disciplinary teams.

Design for fun

As a creative outlet from this work, I messed around in Illustrator and Photoshop creating things for my friends and family (gifts, posters, t-shirt designs, etc). It was fun to learn about typography, color theory, composition and spacing and what not, but it was mostly to create things that got people smiling or excited.

Mixing work and play

I was drawn to UX design as a career because it sounded like this perfect combination of my engineering work and interest in graphic design: designing for utility (pun very much intended) and emotion. In 2019 I enrolled in Bentley’s Human Factors in Information Design program full-time. It gave me a strong foundation in UX principles and helped me learn how to leverage my previous experience through a human-centered lens.

Bonus facts!

The other big part of my life has been ultimate frisbee. I used to play very competitively, but now my lower back hates me so I should really stop. I coach now instead and it's fantastic, which my back appreciates.

I have a dog named Chubbles. His namesake is an abandoned diner in New Hampshire. It's a goofy name for a goofy dog, but it fits.

I've become obsessed with baking the perfect loaf of sourdough bread. I've come pretty close. It still eludes me, but I remain undeterred. I usually bake two loaves at a time, so if you want one let me know.

I'm Jeff. I love designing accessible, useful, & fun experiences for people. Also I like baking bread. Here's more about me.
